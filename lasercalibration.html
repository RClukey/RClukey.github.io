Laser Cutter Calibration Experiment

The Omtech laser cutter the team calibrated by varying laser speed and power.


This project was conducted to demonstrate the engineering experimentation process in the context of problem-solving and design. The primary objective of this project was to determine the optimal laser cutting speed and power settings for 1/8‚Äù acrylic For use by the Rapid Prototyping Studio in Texas A&M University. The experiment aimed to identify settings that maximize fabrication speed and laser cutter longevity.


The ASTM D638 acrylic dog bone specimens the team cut out using the Omtech laser cutter.


Standardized ASTM D638 dog bone specimens were laser cut using varying combinations of speed (30 to 60 mm/s) and power (60% to 70%), then tensile tested using an Instron 68TM-10 benchtop machine. The key mechanical properties measured and compared were modulus of elasticity, ultimate tensile strength, and strain. The team used statistical analysis, particularly two sample t-tests for all two sample combinations of speed and all two sample combinations of power with a 0.05 alpha level.


The results of the t-tests the team ran for comparing the laser cutter speed and power.


These tests revealed no significant differences in strength across the tested parameters. Based on these findings, the recommended settings for routine acrylic cutting are 60 mm/s at 60% power. This balances the rapid prototyping studio's speed, energy efficiency, and equipment longevity.
